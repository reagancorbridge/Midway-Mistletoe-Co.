All About Mistletoe

1. What is Mistletoe?

Mistletoe is a type of parasitic plant that grows on the branches of trees. It has small, oval-shaped green leaves and white berries. The most famous holiday species are Viscum album (European mistletoe) and Phoradendron leucarpum (American mistletoe).

2. Origins and Mythology

Mistletoe has a rich, ancient history:

3. Mistletoe and Christmas

Mistletoe became a Christmas symbol in 18th-century England. Hanging mistletoe represents:

  • Love & romance
  • Peace & goodwill
  • Luck & protection

Families hang it in doorways to invite joy and holiday magic.

4. Where Mistletoe Grows

  • Europe: Apple, oak, and poplar trees.
  • North America: Hardwood trees like oak and hickory.
  • Worldwide: Australia, Africa, and Asia have native varieties.
